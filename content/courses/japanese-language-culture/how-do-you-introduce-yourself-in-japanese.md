# How Do You Introduce Yourself in Japanese?

To introduce yourself in Japanese, say: **"Hajimemashite. Watashi wa [name] desu. Yoroshiku onegaishimasu."** This means "Nice to meet you. I am [name]. Please treat me well." It is the standard formal self-introduction used in Japan.

:::definition
Hajimemashite (はじめまして) is a polite greeting used only when meeting someone for the first time. It literally means "beginning," implying the start of a new relationship.
:::

## Key Phrases for Self-Introduction

### Watashi wa [Name] desu (わたし は \_\_\_ です)

| Term    | Meaning                 |
| ------- | ----------------------- |
| Watashi | I                       |
| wa (は) | topic particle          |
| [Name]  | your name               |
| desu    | polite copula ("am/is") |

:::example
Watashi wa Tanaka desu.
(I am Tanaka.)
:::

:::key
The particle wa (は) marks the topic of a sentence in Japanese. It follows a noun or pronoun and indicates what the sentence is about.
:::

### Yoroshiku onegaishimasu (よろしく おねがいします)

A polite closing phrase with multiple meanings:

- "Please treat me well."
- "I look forward to working/meeting with you."

## Introducing Your Profession

You can replace your name with your profession.

**Pattern:** Watashi wa [profession] desu.

:::example
Watashi wa gakusei desu. (I am a student.)
Watashi wa sensei desu. (I am a teacher.)
Watashi wa bengoshi desu. (I am a lawyer.)
:::

:::aeo
**Sample Full Introduction:**
Hajimemashite. Watashi wa Kumar desu. Watashi wa gakusei desu. Yoroshiku onegaishimasu.

Translation: "Nice to meet you. I am Kumar. I am a student. Please treat me well."

## Asking Questions Using "ka"

To turn a statement into a question, simply add **ka (か)** at the end.

:::comparison
**Statement:**
Watashi wa gakusei desu. (I am a student.)

---

**Question:**
Gakusei desu ka? (Are you a student?)
:::

:::key

- "Ka" (か) makes a sentence interrogative
- Use rising intonation when speaking the question
- Instead of saying anata (you), use the person's name
  :::

:::example
Tanaka-san wa gakusei desu ka?
(Is Tanaka a student?)
:::

## Quick Recap

:::recap

- Use "Hajimemashite" only when meeting someone for the first time
- Structure introductions as "Watashi wa [name/profession] desu."
- Add "ka" (か) at the end of a statement to form questions
- The particle wa (は) marks the topic of your sentence
- Yoroshiku onegaishimasu closes a formal introduction politely
  :::
