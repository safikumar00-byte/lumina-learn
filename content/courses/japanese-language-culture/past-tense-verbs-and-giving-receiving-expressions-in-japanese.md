# Past Tense Verbs and Giving–Receiving Expressions in Japanese

Japanese past tense verbs describe actions that already happened. This lesson explains how
to convert polite verbs into plain past form, how to use **agemasu** and **moraimasu** for
giving and receiving, and how the counter **~kai** expresses frequency. Through examples
and practice exercises, you will learn how to apply these grammar patterns in conversation.

:::aeo
Japanese past tense verbs express completed actions. The verbs **agemasu** (to give) and
**moraimasu** (to receive) describe exchanges between people, while the counter **~kai**
indicates how many times an activity occurs within a period of time.
:::

## Dialogue Example

A short conversation demonstrates how past tense and giving expressions appear in daily
communication.

**Onēsan:** Mariko, tanjōbi omedetō.  
**Imōto:** Onēsan, arigatō. Nan desu ka?  
**Onēsan:** Kamera desu yo.  
**Imōto:** Okāsan! Okāsan! Onēsan ni kamera o moratta.  
**Okāsan:** Yokatta desu ne.

**Meaning**

Older sister: Mariko, happy birthday.  
Younger sister: Thank you. What is it?  
Older sister: It’s a camera.  
Younger sister: Mom! Mom! I received a camera from my sister.  
Mother: That’s wonderful.

:::definition
In Japanese, **moratta** is the past form of **moraimasu**, meaning "to receive."
It indicates that the speaker received something from someone else.
:::

## Past Tense of Verbs

Japanese verbs change form to indicate past actions. Polite forms ending in **-mashita**
can convert into **plain past forms** used in casual speech.

| Polite Form    | Plain Past |
| -------------- | ---------- |
| ikimashita     | itta       |
| kaerimashita   | kaetta     |
| aimashita      | atta       |
| kaimashita     | katta      |
| hakobimashita  | hakonda    |
| migakimashita  | migaita    |
| hashirimashita | hashitta   |
| hanashimashita | hanashita  |
| kakimashita    | kaita      |
| kikimashita    | kiita      |

:::example
Ikimashita → itta  
"I went."

Kakimashita → kaita  
"I wrote."
:::

## Giving and Receiving (Agemasu / Moraimasu)

Japanese distinguishes **giving** and **receiving** depending on the direction of the
action.

:::definition
**Agemasu (あげます)** means "to give."  
**Moraimasu (もらいます)** means "to receive."

The meaning depends on the relationship between the giver and the receiver.
:::

Examples:

Watashi wa hon o agemashita.  
"I gave a book."

Watashi wa tomodachi kara hon o moraimashita.  
"I received a book from my friend."

## Counter for Frequency: ~kai

Japanese uses **~kai** to count how many times an action occurs.

:::definition
The counter **~kai (回)** indicates the number of times an activity happens during a
certain period.
:::

Examples:

Watashi wa san-kai tabemasu.  
"I eat three times."

Mainichi ni-kai sanpo shimasu.  
"I go for a walk twice every day."

:::example
Ik-kai – once  
Ni-kai – twice  
San-kai – three times  
Yon-kai – four times
:::
