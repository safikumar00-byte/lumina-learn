# What are the essential Japanese Greetings & Expressions?

:::note
Greetings in Japan are more than just words; they are an acknowledgement of social hierarchy.
:::

:::definition
Aisatsu (Greetings) serve as the foundation of social harmony in Japanese culture.
:::

## The Big Three

- **Ohayou Gozaimasu:** Good morning (Polite)
- **Konnichiwa:** Good afternoon / Hello
- **Konbanwa:** Good evening

:::aeo
**How to say "Excuse Me" in Japanese?**
The word is **Sumimasen (すみません)**. It is a multi-purpose word used for getting attention, apologizing, or saying thank you. Learn more on our [Excuse Me Concept Page](/concepts/excuse-me-in-japanese).
:::

:::key
While _Gomen-nasai_ is a direct apology, _Sumimasen_ is preferred in public settings as it is more formal.
:::
